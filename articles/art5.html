How Editors, Models and More Styled Their White Tees at the Fashion for Our Future March

By Leah Faye Cooper

March 4, 2024

When I received the call to be part of Rachel Antonoff and Susan Alexandra’s show at New York Fashion Week, I never expected to take on the role of a "show dog." Yes, not just walking the runway, but actually strutting down as if I were a prized pooch on display! The concept of the show was a joyous celebration of playful fashion, and it was unlike anything I had experienced before.

Backstage, the atmosphere was buzzing with excitement. Models were getting their makeup and hair done, stylists were rushing around with accessories in hand, and designers were putting the final touches on their collections. Amidst all the chaos, I was being dressed up in an extravagant outfit, complete with a glittering dog collar adorned with rhinestones.

As the lights dimmed and the music started to play, my heart began to race. When it was finally my turn to walk, I took a deep breath and stepped onto the runway, channeling the graceful movements of a show dog. To my surprise, the audience loved it! Laughter, cheers, and applause echoed as I made my way down the catwalk. For a moment, I forgot I wasn’t a professional model—I was a show dog, and I was absolutely loving it!

The show perfectly captured the whimsical and vibrant spirit of Rachel Antonoff and Susan Alexandra’s designs. From dog-shaped handbags to dresses featuring playful canine motifs, every piece felt like a sweet tribute to our furry friends. But beyond the fashion, the show also celebrated the joy and companionship that pets bring into our lives.

After the show, people came up to compliment me and ask for photos. The playful energy of the event brought smiles and laughter to everyone involved. For me, the experience of being a "show dog" was something I never would have imagined, but I fully embraced the opportunity.

In the end, fashion shows don’t always have to be serious or dramatic. Sometimes, it’s about having fun, embracing the unexpected, and not being afraid to be a little silly. And if that means becoming a "show dog" for a day, I’m more than happy to oblige.

Thank you to Rachel Antonoff and Susan Alexandra for inviting me to be part of this unforgettable moment. And to all the real dogs out there, I hope I made you proud!
